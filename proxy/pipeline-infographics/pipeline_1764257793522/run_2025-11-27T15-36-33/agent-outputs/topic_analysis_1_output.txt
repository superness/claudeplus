## TOPIC_BREAKDOWN

This is not a traditional academic argument topic - it's a **product design and business planning request**. The core components are:

1. **Technical Product Design**: A bundled cryptocurrency mining solution combining node management, stratum server, and web interface
2. **Business Strategy**: Revenue models, go-to-market, competitive positioning
3. **Market Analysis**: Hobbyist mining segment, hardware trends, user personas
4. **Implementation Planning**: MVP scoping, phased roadmap, resource allocation

The scope encompasses full product lifecycle from concept to launch strategy, targeting a niche but growing market of hobbyist cryptocurrency miners.

## CONTROVERSIES

Debatable aspects within this product space:

1. **Open Source vs. Monetization Tension**: How to build community trust while generating revenue
2. **Centralization Risk**: Pre-synced snapshots require trusting the snapshot provider - conflicts with crypto ethos
3. **Multi-coin vs. Focus**: Whether to support many coins (complexity) or go deep on few (market limitation)
4. **Hardware Bundling Ethics**: Selling hardware at markup vs. pure software play
5. **Regulatory Uncertainty**: Mining software in evolving legal landscape

## PERSPECTIVES

Different stakeholders view this differently:

- **Hobbyist Miners**: Want simplicity, low cost, educational value
- **Crypto Purists**: Skeptical of any centralized components (snapshots, hosted services)
- **Investors**: Want clear path to revenue, scalable business model
- **Competitors**: May view as fragmenting already-small market
- **Educators**: Value pedagogical clarity over feature completeness
