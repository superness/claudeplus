- Comprehensive 52-risk register across 6 categories
- Well-prioritized Top 10 risks with detailed mitigation strategies

### Legal/Risk Concerns

1. **International Expansion Gaps**: While EU MiCA is mentioned, no detailed analysis of other key international markets (UK post-Brexit FCA regime, Asian markets) that may be relevant for Year 2-3 expansion

2. **Open Source Liability**: The CLA approach is mentioned but liability for malicious community contributions or supply chain attacks on dependencies deserves more attention given the security-critical nature of mining software

3. **Insurance Exclusions**: Crypto-related exclusions are common in E&O and cyber policies; should note the need to verify coverage explicitly applies to cryptocurrency infrastructure

4. **Emergency Scenario Detail**: Worst case scenario mentions acqui-hire/handoff options but lacks detail on IP transfer, customer migration, or wind-down procedures

### Specific Revisions Required

1. Add a brief section on UK FCA crypto registration requirements (relevant for international expansion)
2. Expand the security vulnerability mitigation to include supply chain security (dependency signing, SBOM generation)
3. Add a note under Insurance Requirements that crypto-related exclusions should be explicitly verified with carriers
4. Add a simplified wind-down checklist in the worst case scenario section

### Verdict

**APPROVED**: The legal and compliance analysis is comprehensive and well-structured. It correctly identifies the critical money transmission architecture requirements, provides actionable compliance roadmaps, and the risk analysis demonstrates appropriate rigor with a practical 52-risk register, clear prioritization, and realistic scenario modeling. The minor gaps identified (international expansion details, supply chain security depth, insurance exclusion verification) are reasonable to address in future iterations as the business scales and do not represent critical omissions for a seed-stage company.

The documents demonstrate strong alignment between legal architecture decisions (no custody, no funds transmission) and risk mitigation strategies. The risk scoring methodology is clear and consistently applied. Budget estimates are realistic and include cost-saving strategies appropriate for a bootstrapped/seed-funded company.

DECISION: approved
